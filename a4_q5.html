A4 Q5 : THE BLOCK BOOTSTRAP

some of the simpler forms of bootstrapping have a sample X, and draw resamplse from X at the individual level

but the block bootstrap is more appropriate when there are many little groups within X, where the errors in each group might be correlated

or if we have time series data where the errors might be correlated, we could break up the data (which might not have any natural blocks or clusters), into our own clusters / blocks of consecutive observations.

i haven't seen anything to rule out partially overlapping blocks

in the case of block bootstrapping, instead of resampling from X, we resample the blocks / clusters of X

if you don't have enough clusters, that's not much different than having a small sample size
